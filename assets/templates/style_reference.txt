INT. DETECTIVE'S OFFICE - NIGHT

Rain against the window. SARAH COLE, 40s, sits across from a desk buried in
files. Her coat is still wet.

The door opens. DETECTIVE RAY MURTAGH, 50s, enters carrying two paper cups.

MURTAGH
Sorry about the wait.

He sets a cup in front of her without asking.

SARAH
You said on the phone you found something.

MURTAGH
We found the car. Torched. Out past the reservoir.

Sarah looks down at her hands. A long beat.

SARAH
Anyone inside?

MURTAGH
No. Just the car.

CUT TO:

EXT. RESERVOIR ROAD - NIGHT

Headlights. A police tape line flaps in the wind. Beyond it, the charred
shell of a sedan.

Murtagh and Sarah stand at the tape. A FORENSIC TECH crouches near the
rear wheel arch, photographing something.

MURTAGH
Paint transfer. Other vehicle hit it before it burned.

SARAH
What colour?

MURTAGH
White. Or light grey. Lab'll know more by morning.

SARAH
(quietly)
He was there.

Murtagh doesn't answer. He already knows.

FADE TO:

INT. SARAH'S APARTMENT - DAWN

The room is dark. Sarah sits on the floor, back against the couch, the
paper cup still in her hand, cold now. She hasn't slept.

Her phone buzzes. She looks at the screen.

INSERT — PHONE SCREEN: a single text message. "Don't look for it."

BACK TO SCENE

Sarah puts the phone face-down on the floor.
